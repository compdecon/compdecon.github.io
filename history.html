History

Please understand that this is a simplified history, there's a lot more to Camp Evans and InfoAge than what we have here.

1912

Marconi builds his receiving station in Belmar NJ (Wall Twp. NJ)

1917

All wireless stations in the US were seized by the US gevernment. Later returned after the armistice.

1941

In 1941 the Belmar radio site was renamed the Evans Signal Laboratory after Wall Township purchased the original Marconi buildings and the surrounding 93 acres for the Army to move the Signal Corps Labs. 1942 the base is dedicated as Camp Evans Signal Laboratory

1999

The 1993 Base Realignment and Closure Commission at the end of the Cold War designated Camp Evans for closure (e.g., the Army Radiation Dosimetry Laboratory closed in 1999). A district of Camp Evans was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2002, and camp areas became part of the Save America's Treasures project.

2011

Dan Wobster creates a makerspace called The Instuture for eXporatory Research (IXR) on the InfoAge Campus, in Wall Township, NJ. The makerspace is named after a Army group that actually existed at Camp Evans. IXR started out in the basement of the Marconi Hotel (InfoAge's main building). New members joined and IXR grew.

CDL originally was a group of InfoAge volunteers who used a portion of building 9059 as a base of operations. This group mowed lawns, painted, set up and dismantled CEBOT, installed network equipment, security cameras, and generally fixed anything that was sent our way. In 2016, CDL built a podcast studio in their space at InfoAge and started using it to market InfoAge, along with providing access to the community.

2012

InfoAge offers the use of part of the Telephone building (building 9059) to IXR, shared with a group called The Computer Deconstruction Lab (CDL).

2018

CDL and IXR merge to become just CDL and applied for a 501c3 (we now have it).

2020

World came to a halt and we all stayed home (pandemic)

2021

We all peeked our head out the door to see if it is okay to come out (I hope it is)/

2022
